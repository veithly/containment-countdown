Required structure:

## Evidence used

Table with source, used for, portable lesson.

## First-pass survival

Cover:
- Required submission fields
- Must-open links
- Fresh-work / version-control requirement
- AI/spec/tool attribution requirement
- Partner/sponsor prize requirement
- Disqualification risks

## Judge personas

Table with at least 4 personas:
- Sponsor technical judge
- Ops/security practitioner
- Product/design judge
- Busy first-pass Devpost judge

For each: what they care about first, what would make them stop watching, evidence we show.

## Winner thesis

Do not choose a project yet. State the thesis the eventual project must satisfy:
- Personal or customer scar
- Why problem is worth attention
- What project must prove in one screen action

- Why it can continue after hackathon

## Prototype cut

State:
- 2-3 must-have features for any selected concept
- One miracle budget
- Cut features

## Attention ladder

Table:
- 0-10s
- 10-30s
- 30-60s
- 2-3min
- 5min / Q&A

## Aha moment

Define required pattern:
- User action
- Visible consequence
- Proof artifact
- Why judge can retell it

## Rubric coverage

Map Splunk judging criteria and bonus prizes to screen/artifact proof.

## Q&A bank

At least 10 likely judge questions with one-sentence answer and proof link/screen.

## Anti-hype red flags

Include ambiguity removed, template/starter-kit distance, missing-code/thin-repo risk, over-indexed criterion, reused-project risk.
